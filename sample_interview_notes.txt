USER RESEARCH NOTES — "FreshBox" meal-kit packaging redesign
Method: 5 remote interviews, 20 min each. Participants are existing subscribers.
(Lightly cleaned transcripts. Names changed.)

--- Participant 1: Riya, 29, works long hours ---
"Honestly the box is huge and I live alone, so I'm throwing out way more
cardboard and ice packs than food I actually eat."
"I never know which meal to cook first. There's no order to it, so sometimes
the fish goes off before I get to it."
"Opening it is fine, but breaking it down for recycling takes forever."

--- Participant 2: Sam, 41, cooks for family of four ---
"The ingredients for different meals are all mixed together. I spend ten
minutes just sorting what belongs to what before I even start."
"The recipe cards get wet and fall apart on the counter."
"I like the portions, but I wish I could tell at a glance what's vegetarian."

--- Participant 3: Aditi, 34, environmentally conscious ---
"The amount of plastic makes me feel guilty every single week. I almost
cancelled over it."
"Those gel ice packs — I have no idea how to dispose of them and they pile up."
"If it were mostly paper and compostable I'd happily pay a bit more."

--- Participant 4: Marcus, 52, not very tech-savvy ---
"I had to scan a QR code to see the full instructions and I just gave up and
guessed."
"Small text on the labels. I need my glasses to read the cooking times."
"Once everything's out of the box I can't tell which bag was for which night."

--- Participant 5: Lena, 26, shares with a flatmate ---
"We split the meals but everything's portioned for the whole box, so dividing
it is annoying."
"The box doesn't fit in our fridge so we leave stuff on the counter, which
worries me with the raw chicken."
"I do love the little spice pouches though — those are clearly labelled and easy."
